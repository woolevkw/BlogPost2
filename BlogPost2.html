How I Got My Own Personal Domain Name For Less Than $15

Everyone should know how to do this!!! Ep. 2

Having a personal domain name to yourself means you can have your own website that can be used for anything. I plan to set up my peronal website as an online portfolio of all the pieces of work I have done while here at Clarkson University. I can then use that portfolio to send to potential employers so they can see all the work I am capable of doing inclucing creating this mini blog series.

Here are the basic steps:

  • First, figure out what you want your domain name to be and/or represent.
    • I used my full name which is perfect for representing my portfolio.
  • Next, figure out where you want to get your domain name from.
    • Domain names are not free so you will have to buy from places such as Google Domains or GODADDY.
    • I personally went through Google Domains.
  • After you have an idea of what you want your domain name to be, you have to run a domain name search to see what the best available option is.
    • No two domain names may be exact. I am lucky enough to have my full name as my domain name because my name is not that common.
  • Pick the best domain name that is available to you.
    • Try to make the name sound as professional as possible.
    • Do not use out dated language and avoid using numbers for words (2, 4, U, etc).
    • Also, make sure the domain name is not too long. People don't want to type in a lot of words when they want to find your site.
    • Lastly, if your domain name is not readable then people will not want to visit your site..
  • Next, you need to figure out what extension you want at the end of your name.
  • The most common extensions are ".com" ".org" ".net" and ".co"
    • This is a difficult part because there are adtantages and disadvateages of all of them.
    • I personally use ".com" because it is the most popular and it was available to me for a cheap price.
  • Now that you have picked out your domain name and extension, move on to purchase it.
    • Custom domain names are not free remember, so you will need your wallet near by.
  • There are going to be some forms that you will need to fill out such as your full name and place of residence.
  • After you fill out the forms and enter your credit card information, you are good to go!
    • If you have any questions or troubles, the site you are trying to get your domain from will have some sort of support you can use for more assistance.

    And there you go! Now you have your own personal Domain Name!
